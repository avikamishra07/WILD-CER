Who We Are

Conservation through Education & Research

Wild-CER (Society for Wildlife Conservation, Education and Research) is a registered non-profit charity focused on the conservation of endangered species through the One Health approach, working closely with the forest department across Central India since 2012.

Founded by Dr. Bahar Baviskar — wildlife veterinarian and IUCN SSC member — we have trained 2,800 frontline forest staff and 1,800 veterinarians while rescuing thousands of wild animals including 17 mammal species, 100 bird species and 15 reptile species.

"Wild-CER is doing wonderful work to conserve three critically endangered species including tiger, sloth bear and vulture."
